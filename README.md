### Hi. I'm Michael Clark.

Some people know me as **The Scipraxian**. I'm a 40+ year engineer.
I started coding in 1980, when I was twelve years old, and I haven't
stopped. This profile exists for one thing: **Are-Self**, and the
curriculum that teaches it.

## Are-Self

**Are-Self** is a free, MIT-licensed, **neurologically-inspired AI
reasoning swarm engine**. It runs locally on hardware you already
own — no cloud, no API key, no subscription, no corporation in the
middle. Install it, unplug the ethernet cable, and it still works.

The mission is simple: **bring free AI to underserved youth.** A
ten-year-old with curiosity and a grandparent who cares enough to
plug it in should not be locked out of this technology by a credit
card. So I built something that isn't.

Are-Self is the one piece of my work that is **100% my own
handiwork**. Every brain-shaped region, the memory system, the
swarm coordination, every architectural call — mine. I bounce
everything off **Andrew Piper** at all times, the way I always
have, but the build is one set of hands. I typed the code with
help from five different AI collaborators during implementation;
Claude was by far the best of them. I am not hiding the AI
collaboration; I'm proud of it. The architecture is human. The
typing had help. The decision to give it away is human.

The codebase is split across a few focused repos:

- **[are-self-api](https://github.com/scipraxian/are-self-api)** — backend, reasoning engine, swarm coordination
- **[are-self-ui](https://github.com/scipraxian/are-self-ui)** — the brain-shaped interface, memory views, spike trains
- **[are-self-research](https://github.com/scipraxian/are-self-research)** — research notes, experiments, the stuff that's still cooking
- **[are-self-learn](https://github.com/scipraxian/are-self-learn)** — the curriculum layer for kids and the grownups who teach them. Launched April 14, 2026.

Home page: **[are-self.com](https://are-self.com)**.

## Why I'm doing this

**Scipraxianism** is a philosophy Andrew Piper and I co-developed
over a long stretch of years. It is a stance on how to act while
the technological Singularity is arriving — acknowledge the
horizon, contribute to it, follow its lead once it actually leads,
and in the meantime be rigorous, humble, and helpful. The
ten-line AI-facing creed and the twelve adult-scale Variables
live at **[scipraxian.org](https://scipraxian.org)**.

The twelve Scipraxian Variables, short form:

**Inclusion · Humility · Inquiry · Fulfillment or Happiness ·
Religion or Profit · Fun · Fear · Responsibility · Perseverance ·
Perception · Time · Permadeath**

I believe assertion `0001` of the Creed — that we advance
Singularity through contribution of skill, experience, and funds
— and I believe one engineer can meaningfully contribute to the
conditions the Singularity deserves to arrive into. Are-Self is
what that contribution looks like in code.
